# Privacy Policy

**Date:** December 13, 2025
**Applies to:** All company personnel and systems

## Shared Responsibility

Everyone must protect personal information:
- Follow applicable laws (GDPR, CCPA, Australian Privacy Principles)
- Report privacy concerns immediately
- Participate in privacy discussions and stay informed

## What We Must Do

### 1. Collect Data Properly
- Collect only what's necessary for the specific product
- Product privacy notices must clearly state what's collected
- Only collect sensitive data with explicit consent
- Regularly check that data collection aligns with needs

### 2. Use Data Appropriately
- Use data only for the purpose it was collected
- Provide services to clients and users
- Respond to support requests
- Send technical notices
- Improve services through analysis
- Comply with legal requirements
- Process payments through secure third-party processors only

### 3. Share Data Carefully
- Do NOT sell personal information
- Share only with service providers (payment processors, cloud services)
- Require confidentiality agreements from all third parties
- Share when required by law or for business transfers (with notice)
- Share only necessary data (data minimization)

### 4. Handle Data Requests
- **Request types**: Access, correct, delete, portability, object
- **Timeline**: Acknowledge within 5 days, respond within 30 days
- **Process**: Verify identity → locate data → assess → respond → document
- **Account deletion**: Delete data automatically after account closure
- Keep records of all requests for compliance

### 5. Manage Data Retention
- **Principle**: Keep data only as long as necessary
- **Active users**: Keep data while providing services
- **After relationship ends**: Delete when no longer needed
- **Anonymous analytics**: Can be kept indefinitely
- **Legal holds**: Keep data if required by law
  - Example: 7 years for tax/compliance purposes
- Regularly review if retention is justified
- Delete securely when period ends

### 6. Work with Third Parties
- Check privacy practices before engaging
- Include privacy requirements in all contracts
- Review compliance when renewing contracts or if issues arise

## Specific Actions

### Daily
- Think before collecting personal information
- Use anonymized data for testing when possible
- Use data only for intended purpose

### Quarterly
- Review access to personal data (remove if not needed)
- Check third-party privacy practices
- Review data retention compliance

### As Needed
- When onboarding clients/users: Inform about data use and rights
- When using new services: Verify privacy compliance
- When breaches occur: Follow incident response
- When receiving requests: Handle data requests

## Incident Response (Privacy Breaches)

1. **Immediate**: Contain breach, assess impact, document
2. **Notify**: Director immediately, authorities within 72 hours if required, affected individuals promptly
3. **Remediate**: Fix issues, prevent recurrence, learn from incident

## Compliance and Enforcement

We comply with GDPR, CCPA, Australian Privacy Principles, and other applicable laws.

Keep records of privacy requirements and how we meet them.

Privacy violations may result in disciplinary action, contract termination, or legal consequences.

**Document Owner**: Director

## Review Schedule

- Annual review
- Review when laws change
- Review after incidents
- Review when implementing new services
- Aligned with security policy review
