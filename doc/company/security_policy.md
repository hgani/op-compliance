## Introduction

This Security Policy establishes the framework for protecting the company's information assets, ensuring business continuity, and maintaining stakeholder trust. It reflects our commitment to implementing robust security controls and practices across all levels of the organization.

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to:

- Protect company information assets from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, alteration, or destruction
- Ensure compliance with legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements
- Maintain the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information
- Establish a consistent approach to security management
- Promote a security-conscious culture throughout the organization

## Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary staff
- All company-owned or leased information systems, networks, and devices
- All information created, stored, processed, or transmitted on behalf of the company
- All company facilities and physical locations
- Third-party service providers and vendors with access to company information or systems

## Policy Statements

### Information Security Management

1. **Information Security Program**: The company shall maintain a comprehensive information security program that addresses all aspects of information security.

2. **Risk Management**: Regular risk assessments shall be conducted to identify, evaluate, and treat information security risks.

3. **Security Governance**: A formal governance structure shall be established to oversee the implementation and maintenance of security controls.

4. **Documentation**: All security policies, procedures, and guidelines shall be documented, regularly reviewed, and updated.

### Access Control

1. **Principle of Least Privilege**: Users shall be granted only the minimum level of access required to perform their job functions.

2. **User Access Management**: Formal processes shall be implemented for granting, reviewing, and revoking user access rights.

3. **Authentication**:
   - All systems requiring authentication shall implement strong authentication mechanisms based on current industry best practices
   - Multi-factor authentication (MFA) shall be required for:
     - Remote access to company systems
     - Privileged accounts
     - Access to sensitive systems and data
   - Password policies shall:
     - Meet or exceed industry-standard complexity requirements
     - Implement reasonable length requirements based on current security guidance
     - Prevent reuse of previous passwords
     - Include appropriate expiration periods based on risk assessment
     - Be reviewed and updated regularly to align with evolving standards

4. **Session Management**: Secure session management practices shall be implemented, including automatic timeout after periods of inactivity.

### Data Protection and Privacy

1. **Data Classification**: All company data shall be classified according to its sensitivity and criticality.

2. **Data Encryption**:
   - All sensitive data shall be encrypted at rest using industry-standard strong encryption algorithms
   - All data transmitted over networks shall be encrypted using current secure protocols
   - Full disk encryption shall be implemented on all company-owned portable devices and where sensitive data is stored
   - Encryption standards shall be reviewed periodically and updated as stronger standards become available

3. **Data Handling**:
   - Clear guidelines shall be established for the handling, storage, and disposal of sensitive data
   - Data retention policies shall be implemented and enforced
   - Secure data disposal methods shall be used

4. **Privacy Compliance**: The company shall comply with all applicable privacy laws and regulations, including GDPR, CCPA, and local data protection requirements.

### Network Security

1. **Network Segmentation**: Networks shall be segmented to restrict access and contain potential security breaches.

2. **Firewall Configuration**: Firewalls shall be configured to deny all traffic by default and only allow necessary services and protocols.

3. **Wireless Security**:
   - All wireless networks shall use current industry-standard encryption protocols
   - Guest networks shall be isolated from the corporate network
   - Wireless intrusion detection/prevention systems shall be implemented where appropriate
   - Wireless security controls shall be reviewed and updated as new threats and technologies emerge

4. **Remote Access**: Secure VPN connections shall be required for all remote access to company resources.

### Application Security

1. **Secure Development**: Secure coding practices shall be followed in all software development activities.

2. **Security Testing**: All applications shall undergo security testing before deployment, including:
   - Static application security testing (SAST)
   - Dynamic application security testing (DAST)
   - Penetration testing

3. **Vulnerability Management**: A formal vulnerability management program shall be maintained to:
   - Regularly scan for vulnerabilities
   - Prioritize and remediate vulnerabilities based on risk
   - Track vulnerability remediation efforts

4. **Change Management**: All changes to production systems shall follow a formal change management process.

### Incident Response

1. **Incident Response Plan**: A comprehensive incident response plan shall be maintained and regularly tested.

2. **Incident Classification**: Incidents shall be classified based on severity and impact to ensure appropriate response.

3. **Reporting**: All security incidents shall be reported promptly to the appropriate security personnel.

4. **Response Procedures**: Clear procedures shall be established for:
   - Incident detection and analysis
   - Containment and eradication
   - Recovery and post-incident review
   - Communication with stakeholders

5. **Documentation**: All incidents shall be documented with lessons learned incorporated into security improvements.

### Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery

1. **Backup and Recovery**:
   - Regular automated backups of all critical systems and data
   - Backups shall be encrypted and stored in secure, geographically diverse locations
   - Regular testing of backup and recovery procedures
   - Recovery Time Objective (RTO) and Recovery Point Objective (RPO) shall be defined for critical systems

2. **Business Continuity Planning**: A business continuity plan shall be maintained to ensure critical business functions can continue during disruptions.

3. **Plan Testing**: Business continuity and disaster recovery plans shall be tested regularly based on risk assessment and criticality of systems.

### Security Awareness and Training

1. **Security Awareness Program**: All employees shall participate in a security awareness program that includes:
   - Initial security training upon hiring
   - Regular refresher training based on risk and threat landscape
   - Ongoing security awareness communications and reminders
   - Periodic phishing simulation exercises
   - Role-specific training for personnel with access to sensitive systems or data

2. **Role-Based Training**: Additional specialized training shall be provided to personnel with specific security responsibilities.

3. **Security Updates**: Employees shall be kept informed of current security threats and protective measures.

### Physical Security

1. **Access Control**: Physical access to company facilities shall be controlled and monitored.

2. **Visitor Management**: All visitors shall be registered, escorted, and monitored while on company premises.

3. **Equipment Security**: All company equipment shall be physically secured and tracked.

4. **Clean Desk Policy**: A clean desk policy shall be enforced to prevent unauthorized access to sensitive information.

### Vendor and Third-Party Management

1. **Vendor Risk Assessment**: All third-party vendors with access to company information or systems shall undergo a security risk assessment.

2. **Contractual Requirements**: Security requirements shall be included in all vendor contracts.

3. **Ongoing Monitoring**: Vendors' security practices shall be regularly monitored and assessed.

4. **Data Processing Agreements**: Data processing agreements shall be established with all vendors processing company data.

## Compliance

The company shall maintain compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards. This includes but is not limited to:

- Data protection and privacy regulations applicable to jurisdictions of operation
- Industry-specific compliance requirements (e.g., PCI DSS for payment processing)
- International security standards and frameworks
- Sector-specific regulations (e.g., healthcare, finance)
- Regional and local compliance requirements

The specific compliance obligations shall be identified, documented, and regularly reviewed as regulations evolve.

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Board of Directors
- Provide oversight of the information security program
- Ensure adequate resources are allocated to security initiatives
- Review significant security incidents and their impact

### Executive Management
- Demonstrate leadership and commitment to information security
- Approve the security policy and significant security investments
- Ensure compliance with legal and regulatory requirements

### Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
- Develop and maintain the information security program
- Monitor security controls and their effectiveness
- Coordinate incident response activities
- Report on security status to executive management

### Information Security Team
- Implement and maintain security controls
- Conduct security assessments and audits
- Manage security tools and technologies
- Investigate security incidents

### System Owners
- Ensure the security of systems under their control
- Implement appropriate security controls
- Participate in risk assessments and incident response

### All Employees
- Comply with security policies and procedures
- Report security concerns and incidents
- Participate in security awareness training
- Protect company information and assets

## Policy Review

This security policy shall be reviewed:

- Annually by the Information Security Team
- Following significant security incidents
- In response to changes in business requirements or threat landscape
- To ensure continued relevance and effectiveness

## Enforcement

Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, and may lead to civil or criminal penalties.
